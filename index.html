What is this?

Speedy Delivre: is a weekly email newsletter that is based on personal experiences with anxiety and how we can overcome the intense feelings that isolate us. There is light at the end of the tunnel and you don't have to go on this journey alone.

We'll get through this together!

Where does the name come from?

The idea was born while watching a rerun of Mr. Rogers' Neighborhood. At the time, I was going through a turbulent period in my life and facing daily anxiety and depression. I couldn't sleep, it hurt to exercise, I had no appetite, and I was losing weight rapidly. As I sat there watching one of my favorite childhood shows, a familiar face popped onto the screen, it was Mr McFeely.

Seeing him come onto the television with his cheerful entrance, "Speedy Delivery", was enough to put me at ease, if only for a moment. There were other moments similar to these that really kept me going during that dark time in my life.

The "re:" comes from the RE: in an email, which stands for reply or response. These emails are my response to the increase in people all over the world who are feeling isolated by anxiety.

Who am I?

My name is André Martinez and I'm a Pacific Northwest designer with a passion for email marketing, travel, and being comfortable with the uncomfortable.

I have an invisible struggle with anxiety, but I’ve come a long way in the decade since my first panic attack. I get nervous on planes, boats, trains, and in cars when I’m not in the driver’s seat. That being said, I’ve been on over thirty flights between 2016–2018, both domestic and international.

While anxiety has been a somewhat scarring part of my life, I wouldn't change it for anything. Anxiety has given me purpose and has actually been the catalyst for some of my closest relationships.

I live by a quote from teacher and author Pema Chödrön: To be fully alive, fully human, and completely awake is to be continually thrown out of the nest.
